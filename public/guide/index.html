1. What Is This?

Onboard is a fully functional web platform that operationalizes a 12-month newcomer socialization framework built around three dimensions:

FIT

Role Clarity

Role, KPIs, org chart, responsibilities

ACE

Task Mastery

Tools, processes, training, competence

TIE

Social Acceptance

Team, relationships, belonging

The platform tracks newcomers across 5 phases:

  1. Arrival (Days 1–30) — Learn the basics, meet the team, get oriented
  2. Integration (Days 31–90) — Deepen understanding, take ownership, first results
  3. Adjustment (Months 4–6) — Work independently, expand network, find your voice
  4. Stabilization (Months 7–9) — Consolidate, refine, grow
  5. Embedding (Months 10–12) — No longer new. Part of the fabric.

It is designed as a consultant-led SaaS tool: the consultant (Admin) sets up each client company, uploads customized activity calendars and check-in schedules, and the client's HR team manages day-to-day onboarding with minimal effort.

7. Pre-Arrival AI Interview — The Research Baseline

Research significance: This interview captures the T0 baseline BEFORE day one. Every construct measured here can be tracked longitudinally against monthly check-in data for 12 months.

A deep qualitative interview (12–15 questions) conducted by Claude AI. The conversation flows naturally — not a rigid questionnaire.

What It Explores (6 Research Constructs):

  1. Expectations (Met Expectations Theory — Wanous)
    What they imagine the job, team, and culture will be like. Creates the baseline for measuring expectation-reality gaps at each check-in.
  2. Career Fit (Person-Job / Person-Organization Fit)
    How this role fits their trajectory. Why they chose this company. What they hope to learn.
  3. Anticipated Embeddedness (Mitchell, Lee & Holtom)
    • Links — Pre-existing connections, network plans
    • Fit — Perceived compatibility with role/org/lifestyle
    • Sacrifice — What they gave up, switching costs
  4. Psychological Contract (Rousseau)
    What they expect beyond salary. What they feel they owe. Deal-breakers.
  5. Social Orientation (Allen & Shanock — Socialization Tactics)
    Proactive vs. passive style. How important work relationships are. Social anxieties.
  6. Prior Experience & Anxiety
    Concrete incidents from past onboardings. Coping strategies. What excites/worries them.

How It Works:

  • Newcomer clicks "Start conversation"
  • Claude AI conducts a warm, natural conversation (NOT a rigid Q&A)
  • AI probes for concrete incidents and emotions
  • Full transcript saved to database
  • Takes about 10–15 minutes

8. Transcript Coding & Analysis

Admin → Company → Newcomers tab → "Code Interview" button

Adapted from the Critical Incident Technique coding engine used in the 360-degree evaluation platform.

What the AI Codes:

Every meaningful passage is coded into one of 7 categories:

CategoryWhat It Captures
ExpectationsWhat they expect about daily work, culture, management style
Career FitCareer trajectory alignment, growth expectations
Embeddedness: LinksPre-existing connections, network plans
Embeddedness: FitPerceived compatibility with role/org
Embeddedness: SacrificeWhat they gave up, switching costs
Psychological ContractMutual obligations, deal-breakers
Social OrientationProactive/passive style, social needs
Prior ExperiencePast onboarding incidents, lessons learned
Anxiety & ExcitementWorries, motivations, emotional readiness

For Each Coded Passage:

  • Exact quote from the transcript
  • What it reveals (1 sentence interpretation)
  • Emotional valence: positive, negative, or neutral
  • Intensity: low, moderate, or high
  • Incident type: concrete incident, projection (future), or reflection

Summary Report Generated:

  • Expectations profile
  • Career fit assessment
  • Embeddedness baseline (links, fit, sacrifice)
  • Psychological contract analysis
  • Social readiness assessment
  • Risk factors for early turnover
  • Protective factors for retention
  • Recommended onboarding actions (3–5 specific)
  • Flight risk assessment (low / moderate / high) with justification

11. Data Architecture

Quantitative:

  • Likert scores (1–5) on 15 items × FIT/ACE/TIE at each monthly check-in
  • Self scores vs. Manager scores (divergence analysis)
  • Activity completion rates by dimension, phase, and time

Qualitative:

  • Pre-arrival interview transcript (T0 baseline)
  • Monthly AI interview transcripts (T1–T12)
  • Manager 1:1 notes, buddy logs, HR review notes

Coded:

  • Pre-arrival coding — 7 constructs with valence, intensity, incident type
  • Monthly analysis — FIT/ACE/TIE assessment with quotes, flight risk

Longitudinal tracking:

  • Dimension scores over time (self + manager)
  • Activity completion curves
  • Expectation-reality gap evolution
  • Embeddedness growth

12. Research Potential

This platform generates publication-ready longitudinal data. Below are paper ideas that leverage the data architecture.
  1. Pre-arrival predictors of socialization outcomes — Do T0 expectations, embeddedness, and psychological contract predict T3/T6/T12 adjustment?
  2. Expectation-reality gaps and turnover — How big does the gap need to be before turnover intention rises?
  3. Embeddedness growth curves — When does embeddedness "lock in"? Is there a critical window?
  4. AI vs. human coding of socialization interviews — Compare AI-coded data against manual coding by trained researchers
  5. Proactive socialization and adjustment speed — Use T0 social orientation to predict TIE score trajectories
  6. The role of buddies — Compare companies with vs. without buddy systems on TIE scores
